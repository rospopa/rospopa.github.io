United States: Modern Monetarism

The purpose of this essay is to articulate the principles and practices of the Federal Reserve Bank in order to identify the factors and policies which determine the future course of the United States economy.

We will begin by taking a look at current macroeconomic trends. We will then determine the relationships between macroeconomic variables and assess how modern monetary policy shapes economic outlooks. Lastly, we will uncover the future course of the United States economy.


Macroeconomic Trends


The Federal Reserve Bank is increasing the money supply via Open Market Operations and interest rates are falling. Treasury yields are decreasing. U.S. GDP growth is sluggish. Manufacturing is shrinking. Wages are stagnant while household prices are rising. U.S. debt hit a record high, so did the stock market.

[1]




The Federal Reserve Bank is using QE money to purchase treasury securities, which causes prices for bonds to rise and yields on those bonds to fall.




[2]




Money is continuing to pour into the equity market despite poor business sentiment and trade uncertainty. Low interest rates make it easier for corporations to issue debt, some of which is then used to conduct buybacks of shares. Financial markets are scattering as the economy slows. Wage growth has come to a hault as firms re-evaluate expectations.
